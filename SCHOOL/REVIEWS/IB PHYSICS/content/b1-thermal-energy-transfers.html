B.1 Thermal Energy Transfers

Key Concepts

Temperature and thermal energy are related but distinct concepts in thermodynamics.

Methods of Heat Transfer

Heat can be transferred through three main mechanisms:

  • Conduction: Direct transfer through matter
  • Convection: Transfer through fluid movement
  • Radiation: Transfer through electromagnetic waves

Black-Body Radiation

A black body is an idealized object that:

  • Absorbs all incident radiation
  • Is a perfect emitter of radiation
  • Emits radiation with a characteristic spectrum that depends only on temperature

Stefan-Boltzmann Law

The total power radiated by a black body is given by:

$$P = \sigma AT^4$$

Where:

$P$ = power radiated (W)

$\sigma$ = Stefan-Boltzmann constant ($5.67 × 10^{-8}$ W/m²·K⁴)

$A$ = surface area (m²)

$T$ = absolute temperature (K)

Wien's Displacement Law

The wavelength of maximum intensity is given by:

$$\lambda_{max}T = b$$

Where:

$\lambda_{max}$ = peak wavelength (m)

$T$ = absolute temperature (K)

$b$ = Wien's displacement constant ($2.9 × 10^{-3}$ m·K)

Intensity and Distance

The intensity of radiation follows an inverse square law:

$$I = \frac{P}{4\pi d^2}$$

Where:

$I$ = intensity (W/m²)

$P$ = power radiated (W)

$d$ = distance from source (m)
